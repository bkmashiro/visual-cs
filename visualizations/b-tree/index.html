How It Works

This page uses a B-Tree of order 4 (minimum degree 2). Every node stores up to three keys and four children.

  • Insertion splits any full child on the way down so the recursive descent never enters a full node.
  • Deletion borrows from siblings when possible, otherwise merges two minimum children around a separator key.
  • Search follows the separator keys until it finds the target or reaches a leaf.
