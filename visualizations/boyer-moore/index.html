How Boyer-Moore Works

BM aligns the pattern at a shift, then scans right-to-left. On a mismatch, it takes the maximum of two heuristic shifts:

Bad Character: Move pattern so the mismatched text character aligns with its rightmost occurrence in the pattern. If absent, skip past it entirely.

Good Suffix: The already-matched suffix t must reappear elsewhere in pattern, or a prefix of pattern must match a suffix of t.

Taking max(bc, gs) gives O(n/m) best case — often sub-linear on natural text!
