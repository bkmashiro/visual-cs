🧠 How It Works

Rabin-Karp hashes the pattern and each length-m window of the text. Most shifts only compare patternHash and windowHash.

If the hashes differ, slide in O(1) with a rolling hash. If the hashes match, verify characters to rule out collisions.

The rolling update removes the outgoing character, multiplies by base, adds the incoming character, then applies mod.

⚡ Complexity

AverageO(n + m)
Worst caseO(nm)
SpaceO(1)

Worst case happens when many windows share the same hash and require full verification.
